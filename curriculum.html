Launch Program Curriculum

Launch is an immersive 11-week program, split into 3 parts:

  1. Deep skills training
  2. Industry exposure and network building
  3. Launching a real project

You'll meet at HackCville's awesome coworking spaces each weekday 9:30am-5:30pm starting on May 29 and ending on August 4. (Learn more about our fun and functional spaces here.)

Part 1/3: Deep Skills Training

You'll pick one of two tracks to immerse yourself in, each led by a cohort of expert instructors.

Track A: Full Stack Development

Our Full-Stack Development Track will move you up the software engineering ladder in a fraction of the time of a traditional computer science program. You'll learn through fun, challenging, real hands-on projects - no theoretical stuff here. Comparable programs can cost upwards of $10,000 for just this portion of our program. No former computer science experience is required.

Here's what you'll learn.

This is an intensive program to turn an individual into a competent, professional software developer. The skills learned in this program will extend far beyond web or mobile development. Trainees learn the ins and outs of the internet, how to design for millions, how to stay up to date with the latest tools, and how to present yourself and sell your ideas.

Here's what you'll walk away with.

This program will include the development of several portfolio-worthy applications. It will ensure a basic understanding of client interactions and consulting. Lastly, you'll be able to take an idea from abstract to implementation in a team environment. It is our aim not only to teach you to make your ideas happen, but also to be both employable and billable as a software development consultant immediately following this program.

Track B: Design & Digital Marketing

"The Valley skill set that should be in highest demand and greatest scarcity is neither engineering nor design, but rather internet marketing." - Dave McClure, Founder, 500Startups

In the Full Stack Marketing Track we aim to teach a combination of art, design, tools, and strategies that make digital marketing a compelling field for study and employment. You'll get trained on the marketing concepts used by some of the most compelling internet companies by people who've done it. Comparable programs cost $10,000 just for this portion of the program.

Here's what you'll learn.

This is an intensive program centered on teaching the tools and mindset you'll need to launch real, integrated, digital marketing programs. You'll learn how marketing is combined with product development, usability, and customer lifetime value to create amazing programs that cut through clutter and make a difference.

Here's what you'll walk away with.

You'll leave with the tools and tactics to launch online products. You'll be well versed in the Adobe Creative Suite, email marketing software, marketing concepts and jargon, lifetime customer value analysis, data analysis, social media campaign creation and management, search engine optimization, and growth hacking strategies. It is our aim that after completing this program you could be a key member of a startup marketing team or well paid digital marketing consultant.

Part 2/3: Industry Exposure

A key differentiator for the Launch program is its integration with local industry experts in 3 key industries.

Something we hear a lot at HackCville is that students desperately want to learn about the structure, problems, and possible opportunities in various industries. Thus, every day for 3 weeks, you'll use the afternoons to learn as much as you can from local industry veterans and startups in the Healthcare, Energy, and Food industries. Twice per week, an expert design thinking facilitator will lead deep dives into specific problems or ideas elicited during these sessions.

Health

Healthcare is a trillion dollar industry on the verge of seismic change. Data, new tools, research, and new sensing technologies lead to the creation of dozens of new startups monthly.

Energy

Climate change is real. Finding and distributing critical new sources of energy and optimizing current sources is one of the top issues of the 21st century.

Food

Our nation's food system is large, inefficient, and undergoing tectonic shifts. Data, robotics, new production processes, and new understandings of food science converge to somehow feed our growing population.

Part 3/3: Starting a Project

This is what you've been building to. You'll use your new skills and knowledge to create a new venture.

Launch Part 3 is designed to teach you to start like an expert entrepreneur. Now that you've got some of the basics down, you'll learn the venturing process from world renowned entrepreneurial scholars and practitioners.

Here's what you'll learn.

Launch will teach you to grow an idea into a venture through bootstrapping, customer development, de-risking, and building a network of committed stakeholders. We aim to radically change your mindset to handle the ambiguity of starting any new idea. Specifically, we'll drill down to push ideas created by you and your cohort towards viability.

Here's what you'll walk away with.

You'll leave Launch with a toolkit to start any venture like an entrepreneur. Based on the maturity of your project, you may 1) have the beginnings of an awesome venture you can work on over the next couple years, 2) quit school to start the next Facebook, or 3) have an amazing example of your skills for an employer.
